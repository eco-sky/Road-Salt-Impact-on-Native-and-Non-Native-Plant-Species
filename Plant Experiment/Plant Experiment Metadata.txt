METHODOLOGICAL INFORMATION
---------------------------


1. Description of methods used for collection/generation of data:

Data were generated through a controlled plant growth experiment examining the effects of road salt on Western Yarrow and White Dutch Clover from February 18 to March 23, 2026. Seeds were grown in pots containing potting medium with four road salt concentrations, with 24 replicates per treatment, resulting in 96 pots per species and 192 pots total. Plants were maintained under controlled light and watering conditions. Germination and plant height were recorded throughout the experiment, with daily measurements taken during early growth (days 1 to 9) and weekly measurements thereafter. Plant height was measured from the soil surface to the tallest leaflet, and germination was recorded as the first observed sprout per replicate. Pots were sown with a few seeds to increase germination success, and salt concentrations were designed to simulate roadside runoff conditions using Windsor Ice Melt containing NaCl and CaCl₂.


2. Methods for processing the data:

Raw measurements of plant height and germination were manually entered into Excel, organized by pot ID, species, treatment, road salt concentration, and day of measurement. Derived variables, such as growth rate (height/day starting on day 9), were calculated systematically in Excel. The Plant Growth Summary Statistics file was generated using JMP (Student Edition 19).


3. Instrument- or software-specific information needed to interpret the data:

Growth lights used:

	Sondiko LED Grow Lights – red and blue light settings used to promote consistent plant growth during the experiment.

Scale used:

	Scientific scale – borrowed from Simon Fraser University (SFU) for weighing seeds (<0.1 g).


Software used for statistical summary and analysis:

	JMP Student Edition 19

Other software/apps used:
	
	Excel (Microsoft 365) for data entry, organization, and calculating growth rate.


4. Standards and calibration information, if appropriate: None


5. Environmental/experimental conditions:

The experiment was conducted under controlled indoor conditions from February 18 to March 23, 2026. Plants were grown in pots with potting medium mixed with four concentrations of Windsor Ice Melt, designed to simulate roadside runoff conditions and containing both NaCl and CaCl₂. Treatment groups consisted of 0 g (control), 0.0841 g (low), 0.1683 g (moderate), and 0.2525 g (high) road salt per 100 g soil, based on chloride mass fractions. Each treatment group included 24 replicate pots, resulting in 96 pots per species and 192 pots in total. Two Sondiko LED grow lights with red and blue settings provided consistent light, automatically on from 7:56 AM to 7:56 PM each day, and plants were watered three times per week. Pots were sown with a few seeds to increase germination success. Temperature and humidity were maintained within typical indoor ranges, and thinning was performed on day 17 to prevent overcrowding.


6. Describe any quality-assurance procedures performed on the data:

Data were recorded carefully and manually entered into Excel. Entries were checked for consistency, and outliers (e.g., dead plants with growth rate = 0) were identified and excluded from growth rate analyses. Derived variables, such as growth rate, were calculated systematically using consistent formulas.


7. People involved with sample collection, processing, analysis and/or submission:

All principal investigators were involved in sample collection. Laura primarily conducted the experiment, including watering plants, measuring plant height, and entering data into Excel. Lucas contributed to data analysis using JMP. Sena managed data submission and organization in Excel, including preparing the metadata documentation.
